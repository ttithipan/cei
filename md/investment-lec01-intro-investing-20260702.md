## 1. What is an Investment? Why Invest?

**Investment** is the use of accumulated money to generate higher returns than savings — investing in assets or instruments such as stocks, mutual funds, real estate, cryptocurrency, gold, collectibles, NFTs, etc.

> **Investing is riskier than saving.**

### How to Reduce Investment Risk

1. Possess investment knowledge
2. Understand yourself
3. Discipline in investment (cutting losses)
4. Have a good investment plan

## Source of Income

> 📄 See [PDF page 29](documents/investment-lec01-intro-investing-20260702.pdf#page=29) — income sources diagram.

There are broadly two types of income:

| Type | Description | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Active Income** | You trade time for money — stop working, income stops | Salary, freelance, business profits you personally run |
| **Passive Income** | Money works for you — income flows without active daily effort | Dividends, rental income, interest, royalties, capital gains |

The goal of investing is to build **passive income streams** so you're not dependent on trading your time for money forever.

> 💡 **Getting rich before old age, or get old before you get rich?** — The earlier you start investing, the more time compound interest has to work in your favour.

### The Financial Pyramid / Triangle

A common framework for prioritising your money:

```
         ┌──────────┐
         │  GROWTH   │  ← Invest for wealth (stocks, mutual funds, real estate)
         ├──────────┤
         │PROTECTION │  ← Insurance (health, life) + emergency fund (6–12 months)
         ├──────────┤
         │  SAFETY   │  ← Cash reserves, bank deposits, money market
         └──────────┘
```

1. **Safety layer:** Build cash reserves first (3–6 months of expenses in liquid savings)
2. **Protection layer:** Get adequate insurance coverage + 6–12 months emergency fund
3. **Growth layer:** Invest remaining funds for long-term wealth creation

## Summary

Investment is to **delay spending today to buy assets**. It aims to receive **cash flow** and **value increase** — to be worth the opportunity cost, compensate for inflation, and compensate for risk.
