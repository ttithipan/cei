## What is a System Administrator?

A person who is responsible for the upkeep, configuration, and reliable operation of computer systems, especially multi-user computers, such as servers. Often shortened as **sysadmin**.

The system administrator seeks to ensure that the uptime, performance, resources, and security of the computers they manage meet the needs of the users, without exceeding a set budget when doing so.

— [Wikipedia: System administrator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/System_administrator)

## What is System Administration?

System administration is the field of work in which someone manages one or more systems, be the hardware, software, and related processes. The goal is ensuring the systems are running efficiently and effectively.

### Who is Responsible for System Administration?

System administration is typically done by information technology experts for or within an organization.

> **You're supposed to be an IT expert who is good at system administration!!**

## Sysadmin Tasks

| Task Area | Description |
|---|---|
| **Fault Management** | Detection, logging, and resolution of system problems and faults. May attempt to auto-correct faults. |
| **Configuration Management** | Tracking hardware and software configuration, network topology, software versions. |
| **Security Management** | Authentication, access control, policy definition and enforcement, exception reporting. |
| **Performance Management** | Monitoring availability, response time, error rate, throughput, and utilization. |
| **Accounting Management** | Asset control (knowing what computers are on the network and who uses them) and cost management. |

## How Should We Learn Sysadmin?

- Sysadmins often lack a precise job description.
- Best learn through practical experience.
- Learning all the technical topics related to sysadmin is too much.
- Within this course, we will learn the practice of sysadmin primarily through fundamentals of Linux system administration.
