# CEI Orientation — Year 3 & Year 4 Roadmap

> Organised from voice recording — July 2026 orientation.

---

## Overview of Required & Elective Components

| Component | When | Credits | Grade | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **CEPP** | Y3S2 | 1 | A/B/C | 1 semester |
| **Internship** | After Y3S2 (break) | 0 | S/U | ≥2 months |
| **Cooperative Study** | Y4S1 | 6 | A/B/C | ≥4 months |
| **Capstone Project 1** | Y4S1 | 3 | A/B/C | 1 semester |
| **Capstone Project 2** | Y4S2 | 3 | A/B/C | 1 semester |
| **Oversee Training** | Y4S1 | 6 | A/B/C | 1 semester |
| **Study Abroad** | Y4S1 | 6 | A/B/C | 1 semester |

- **CEPP and Internship are mandatory for everyone.**
- For Y4S1 you must pick **one** of: Cooperative Study, Capstone Project, Oversee Training, or Study Abroad.
- Coop and Capstone are the two main tracks; you do **either** coop **or** capstone (not both).

---

## 1. CEPP (Computer Engineering Preliminary Project)

**When:** Y3S2, but you can start preparing now (July).

- Group of **3** (can be more).
- Choose a topic, then find an advisor.
- Meet with advisor weekly or regularly.
- Submit a proposal/report at the end of the semester.
- At the end, give a presentation and receive comments.
- **1 credit** (according to orientation, 2 credits in the curriculum document), graded A/B/C.
- Think of it as a "mini capstone" — a project simulation.
- A Google Classroom for CEPP will be announced soon.

> If someone in your group later decides to go for Coop instead of Capstone, that is fine — groups can be rearranged later.

---

## 2. Internship

**When:** After Y3S2 (summer break, not overlapping with a regular semester).  
**Duration:** Minimum **8 weeks (2 months)**.  
**Credits:** 0 (required but no credits), graded **S/U**.  
**Registration:** Opens in April. No tuition fee.

### Deadlines

- Submit internship report and diary by **15 July** (15 days to prepare after finishing).
- All paperwork must be submitted before going.

### Section IDs (register in the correct group!)

| Section ID | Who |
|---|---|
| 901 | 2nd & 3rd year students |
| 902 | 4th year students expected to graduate |
| 903 | International internship / exchange abroad |

> **Registering in the wrong group = Grade I (Incomplete).**

### Documents — Internship

1. Fill **SI101** and **SI201**.
2. Combine with: student ID, resume, transcript.
3. Send to **Dr. Kanut** for signature.
4. Then send to **P'Ploy** via email.

Student Affairs will issue an **acceptance form**. You must:
- Ask the company to fill it in.
- Return it to Student Affairs.
- Student Affairs will then send the official internship placement letter to the company.
- *You may need to juggle forwarding documents between parties.*

### During Internship

- Keep a **diary** with daily time-in / time-out.
- Report weekly or regularly on what you did.
- Maximum **3 days sick and personal leave** (requires medical certificates submitted with the diary).
- 3 late arrivals = 1 absence.
- Do **not** take company secrets outside — write something generic in the diary if needed.
- Work schedule depends on the company (6-day work week, holidays, etc.).

### Internship + Coop at Same Company

If you do both at the same company:
- Internship: 2 months (SI documents).
- Coop: 4 months (COOP documents).
- **Keep the documents completely separate.**
- Total = 6 months.

## 3. Cooperative Study (Coop)

**When:** Y4S1  
**Duration:** 4 months (full semester).  
**Credits:** 6 (equivalent to Capstone 1 + 2).  
**Grade:** A/B/C.

### Constraints

- If you register for Coop, Oversee Training, or Study Abroad, you **cannot** register for any other courses.
- No leave exceeding **10%** of total work time.

### Documents — Coop

Before going:
1. Find a company and get their acceptance.
2. Fill **COOP 101, 102, 103**.
3. Send to **Prof. Kanut** to sign.
4. Combine with transcript and student ID.
5. Send to **P'Ploy**.

An acceptance form will be issued; you must:
- Have the company sign and return it to you.
- Return it to the university.

During and after Coop:
- During: submit **COOP 104, 105**.
- After: submit a **report** and **poster** (a format/template will be provided).
- The CE department may require you to **present** the project you did with the company.
- There will be a **university visit** at the company.

### Timeline

- Around July, P'Ploy will combine documents and send them to the Line group.
- Some files come from Student Affairs after you submit COOP 101/102.
- Grades are announced around **December or January** after assessment.

## 4. Capstone Project

**When:** Y4S1 (Capstone 1) and Y4S2 (Capstone 2).  
**Credits:** 3 + 3 = 6 total.  
**Grade:** A/B/C.

- You can **continue** your CEPP plan into Capstone 1.
- **Capstone 1:** Submit report and do presentations at the end of the semester.
- **Capstone 2:** Harder than Capstone 1; includes a **demonstration** and a **final report**.
- If doing capstone with a professor, you **can** also register for GenEd courses.

---

## 5. Other Y4S1 Options

### Oversee Training

- Credits can be transferred to: internship, free GenEd, possibly Coop.
- Can be done in Thailand or abroad.

### Study Abroad

- Rare option; most students do Coop or Capstone.

---

## 6. Supervisor Requirement

In Y4, **regardless of which track you choose** (Capstone, Coop, Oversee Training, or Study Abroad), you will need a **supervisor** to assess your grade.
